Our Story

From a small idea to a thriving, supportive community for students on campus.

Our Origins

As multi-cultural graduate students, the muse. founders came together to reflect on the social problem felt closest to our lived experience and the gaps we saw in our communities.
Moving countries, leaving everything familiar behind, and starting anew in a foreign land is a daunting, complex challenge. We felt we understood on a personal level the unique struggles that international students face, from cultural adjustments to academic and financial pressures.

International university students are more vulnerable on account of social isolation, exploitation in employment, insecure housing, financial instability and racism/discrimination. There is a persistent stereotype in Australian media that international students are all exclusively from high-income families, but this is far from the truth. This assumption overlooks those from low-income backgrounds who are just as likely to experience financial hardship. Financial strain is a major source of psychological stress for some of these students.

International students, however are not a monolith. Amongst this large subsection, different ethnicities, cultural and financial backgrounds and language proficiencies affect a student’s experiences overseas.

Our Mission

muse. was born out of a desire to create a safe, inclusive space for minority international students, but also to provide flexible employment opportunities to help ease financial burdens. The virtual assistant agency is also a significant way to utilise grad student's skills and experience to help other local small businesses and entrepreneurs thrive. We are dedicated to empowering minority international students. Our mission is to create an inclusive environment where everyone can thrive socially, academically and personally.
